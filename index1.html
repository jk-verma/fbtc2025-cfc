Federated Blockchain—Trends and Challenges

Call for Chapters | Book to be Published with Bentham Science Publishers
Submission Deadline: 15 August 2025
Submit Your Chapter via Microsoft CMT

FBTC-2025 is an upcoming edited volume titled "Federated Blockchain: Trends and Challenges", which aims to bring together pioneering research, practical case studies, and interdisciplinary innovations at the intersection of federated learning and blockchain technology. The book will be published by Bentham Science, a globally recognized academic publisher.

As organizations move toward secure, collaborative, and privacy-preserving data systems, federated blockchain is emerging as a game-changing paradigm. This book invites contributions that explore the architecture, applications, challenges, and future research directions of federated blockchain technologies across domains such as AI, Industry 5.0, digital twins, and privacy-centric data ecosystems.

The proposed book seeks to address both foundational theories and advanced implementations, making it a valuable resource for academics, researchers, practitioners, and policymakers involved in distributed systems, cybersecurity, and intelligent infrastructure.

Submission Guidelines

We invite original and unpublished chapter proposals for Federated Blockchain—Trends and Challenges, to be submitted through our Microsoft CMT portal.

Submission Process

  • All submissions must be made via Microsoft CMT: [Click Here for Submission]
  • First-time users must create a Microsoft CMT account before submitting.
  • Each submission must include the following:
    • Title of the chapter
    • Author(s) details with affiliation and email
    • Abstract (150–250 words)
    • 3–5 keywords
    • Full chapter manuscript in Microsoft Word (.docx) format

Formatting Requirements

  • Length: 6,000–8,000 words (including references, tables, and figures)
  • Font: Times New Roman, 12 pt
  • Line Spacing: 1.5
  • Margins: 1 inch on all sides
  • Reference Style: APA 7th Edition
  • Use numbered section headings (e.g., 1., 1.1., 1.2.)

Chapter Structure

  1. Chapter Title
  2. Author Details (Name, Affiliation, Email, ORCID)
  3. Abstract and Keywords
  4. Introduction
  5. Main Body with appropriate subsections
  6. Case Studies/Use Cases (if applicable)
  7. Challenges and Future Scope
  8. Conclusion
  9. References

Review Process

All chapters will undergo a double-blind peer review. Authors may be asked to revise their submissions based on reviewer comments before final acceptance.
